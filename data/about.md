## Engineering Philosophy

**Test everything, trust nothing.**
10 years in QA taught me that the only reliable system is a well-tested system. I apply this discipline to everything — blockchain infrastructure, AI pipelines, backend services. If it's not validated end-to-end, it's not production-ready.

**AI as engineering infrastructure.**
The AI in my systems does a specific job: analyze build failures, retrieve engineering context, compare contracts against vulnerability patterns, orchestrate test execution. It's not decoration — it's infrastructure that makes engineering teams more effective.

**Understand the protocol.**
You can't build reliable blockchain testing infrastructure by copying patterns. I've studied Substrate pallet mechanics, EVM state transitions, cross-chain bridge protocols, and Uniswap V3 internals because that depth is what separates test suites that catch real bugs from test suites that give false confidence.
