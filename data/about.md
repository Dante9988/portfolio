## Engineering Philosophy

**AI as infrastructure, not decoration.**
The AI in my systems does a specific job: synthesize signals that are too complex for deterministic rules.
Everything else — data pipelines, validation, retry logic, observability — is conventional software engineering
done well. That's the difference between AI that works in production and AI that works in demos.

**Understand the protocol.**
You can't build reliable blockchain infrastructure by copying patterns. I've studied Pump.fun bonding curves,
Substrate pallet mechanics, EIP-712 signature flows, and Jito bundle internals because that depth is what
separates systems that handle edge cases from systems that fail silently in production.

**Own the full system.**
When I build an AI analytics platform, I also own the data ingestion pipeline, the vector DB schema, the
scoring model logic, the API layer, and the test infrastructure. That end-to-end ownership is what produces
reliable systems rather than fragile duct-taped ones.

**Test everything that matters.**
In blockchain and trading systems, bugs can be irreversible. That's why I build test infrastructure alongside
production code — not as an afterthought.
