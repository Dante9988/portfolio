## Who I Am

I'm a Software Engineer who builds at the intersection of AI systems, blockchain infrastructure, and backend engineering. Over the past decade my career has followed a deliberate arc: from automation and reliability engineering into distributed backend systems, then deep into blockchain protocol work, and most recently into AI systems engineering — building RAG pipelines, LLM orchestration layers, and real-time intelligence platforms.

That progression matters because each phase built on the last. The automation discipline taught me how to design systems that fail gracefully. The blockchain work taught me distributed consensus, cryptographic verification, and on-chain/off-chain coordination. And the AI work brings it together: building production systems where LLMs, vector retrieval, and real-time data pipelines need to operate reliably at scale.

I don't treat AI as a product feature. I treat it as infrastructure — embedding LLMs where they genuinely solve hard problems (intent parsing, contextual retrieval, multi-source signal synthesis), and keeping everything else deterministic and observable.

## Engineering Philosophy

**AI as infrastructure, not decoration.**
The AI in my systems does a specific job: synthesize signals that are too complex for deterministic rules. Everything else — data pipelines, validation, retry logic, observability — is conventional software engineering done well.

**Understand the protocol.**
You can't build reliable blockchain infrastructure by copying patterns. I've studied Pump.fun bonding curves, Substrate pallet mechanics, EIP-712 signature flows, and Jito bundle internals because that depth is what separates systems that handle edge cases from systems that fail silently.

**Own the full system.**
When I build an AI analytics platform, I also own the data ingestion pipeline, the vector DB schema, the scoring model logic, the API layer, and the deployment infrastructure. End-to-end ownership produces reliable systems.
